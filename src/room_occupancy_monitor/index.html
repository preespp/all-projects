Room Occupancy Monitor

Embedded Firmware Device

Computer Engineer

Project Description

Room Occupancy Monitor is a multidisciplinary engineering design 4-memberproject built to monitor how many people enter and leave a room and prevent overcrowding once the maximum occupancy is reached. The project combined mechanical design, electronics, embedded firmware, and client-focused product development.

I worked on the Electrical and Computer Engineering side of the project, focusing on firmware, IoT capabilities, testing, and coordination across the team. It was one of my earliest experiences building a product for a real client need instead of just a class requirement.

My Responsibilities

  • Built a room capacity monitor that used sensor data and a mechanical actuator to prevent overcrowding.
  • Optimized component selection to reduce the client's required cost by 41%.
  • Programmed firmware in C++ and added ESP32-based IoT capability through UART communication.
  • Performed electrical wiring, assembly, and testing and helped manage weekly tasks and reports.

Skills I Learned

  • Embedded firmware and hardware integration.
  • Cost-aware design and client-centered engineering.
  • Cross-disciplinary teamwork and project management.
  • Testing and validating a full physical prototype.
